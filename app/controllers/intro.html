Piano Trivia

  • Bartholomeu Christophori is credited for inventing the Piano around the turn of the 18th century. He was an inventor and a musician for an Italian magistrate who wanted an instrument that was similar to the harpsicord, but that could fluctuate in volume. It took more than a decade and seven different renditions before Christophori had a design that he and other musicians were pleased with. He named his new instrument the Piano Forte ...Italian for Soft Loud.
  • Christophori's design for has gone mostly unchanged to this day... more than 200 years later.
  • The upright piano became popular starting in the 1890's, and by 1910 well over 100 different piano manufacturers were selling pianos in the United States. However, during the first and second world wars the majority of those piano manufacturers converted to making wartime goods, or were forsed to close altogether.
  • It wasn't until the 1940's that the piano industry experienced a resurgence with the production of a cheaper, smaller piano called the Spinet, or Drop Action piano. By the 1960's the piano had become an american staple for in-home entertainment.
  • Today piano technicians are called on to service all types of pianos, from brand new concert grands to family heirlooms from 1895.
